Effect of p and r - where are steady states?

p is ratio of proliferation rates, Rate(E)/Rate(M). Ranges from 0 to 2. r is rate of conversion from E to M. Ranges from 0 to 1. q is zero.

set(0,'defaultfigureposition',[1300 10 800 800])

hold off;
ststs=zeros(21,21);
for j=1:1:21
    subplot(3,7,j);
    xlabel('time','FontSize',14)
    ylabel('E/(E+M)','FontSize',14)
    p=j*0.1-0.1;
    title(['p=' num2str(p)])
    axis([0,50,0,1])
    hold on;
    for k=1:1:21
        r=k*.05-.05;
        E0=0.5;         %(initial rate of change of
        M0=0.5;           %E and M don't appear to matter)
        tspan=[0 50];
        y0=[4,6];      %(if both start out as zero weird things happen initially)
        yp0=[E0;M0];
        option = odeset('RelTol', 10^-4);
        [t,y]=ode15i('example1', tspan, y0, yp0, option, r, p);
        prop=[y(:,1)]./([y(:,1)]+[y(:,2)]);
        semilogy(t,prop)
        ststs(j,k)=prop(end);
    end
    hold off;
end

Effect of r and p - where are steady states?

r is rate of conversion from E to M. Ranges from 0 to 1. d is ratio of proliferation rates, Rate(E)/Rate(M). Ranges from 0 to 2.

clf;
hold off;
ststs=zeros(21,21);
for j=1:1:21
